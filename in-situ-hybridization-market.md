<p><strong>The In Situ Hybridization Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization (FISH)</li><li>Chromogenic In Situ Hybridization</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The in situ hybridization market comprises two main types: Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization (FISH) and Chromogenic In Situ Hybridization. FISH utilizes fluorescently labeled probes to detect specific nucleic acid sequences within fixed tissues or cells, providing a vivid visualization of gene localization and abnormalities, crucial in research and clinical diagnostics. In contrast, Chromogenic In Situ Hybridization employs enzyme-based detection systems to produce a colored reaction product, allowing for easier interpretation under a light microscope, particularly in histopathology. Both techniques are vital in genetics and cancer research.</p></p>
