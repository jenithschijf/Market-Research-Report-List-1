<p><p>The passenger vehicle aluminum alloy wheel market comprises various manufacturing types, including casting, forging, and other methods. Casting involves pouring molten aluminum into molds, allowing for complex designs and shapes, making it popular for mass production. Forging, on the other hand, uses high pressure to shape heated aluminum, resulting in stronger and more durable wheels. Other methods may include extrusion and machining processes. Each type offers different benefits in terms of strength, weight, cost, and design flexibility.</p></p>
