<p><p>The supercapacitor market features various types: Double Layer Capacitors (DLCs) utilize electrostatic charge separation; Pseudocapacitors use fast electron transfer for energy storage; Hybrid Capacitors combine features of supercapacitors and batteries for enhanced performance. Composite Hybrids integrate materials for improved capacitance and stability, while Asymmetric Hybrids have different electrodes to optimize energy and power density. Battery-type Hybrids aim to merge the high energy density of batteries with the rapid charge and discharge capabilities of supercapacitors, catering to diverse energy storage needs.</p></p>
