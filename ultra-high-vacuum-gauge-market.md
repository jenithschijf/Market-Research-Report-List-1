<p><p>The Ultra High Vacuum (UHV) gauge market consists primarily of two types: Cold Cathode Vacuum Gauges and Hot Cathode Vacuum Gauges. Cold cathode gauges use a low-pressure discharge to measure vacuum levels, making them suitable for high vacuum conditions and requiring minimal maintenance. In contrast, hot cathode gauges operate at elevated temperatures, ionizing gas molecules to provide precise measurements at very low pressures. Both types are essential for various applications in industries such as semiconductor manufacturing and scientific research, offering reliability and accuracy.</p></p>
